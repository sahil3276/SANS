## Your corrected timeline

| Date | What happens |
|------|--------------|
| Jan 1 | Company buys. SANS portal access starts. GIAC attempt shows as "available" but not yet activated |
| Jan 2–4 | OnDemand training |
| Jan 5 – Feb 14 | Build index, redo labs, study from courseware (no practice tests yet) |
| **~Feb 15** | **You click "Activate" in the GIAC portal** ← 120-day clock starts |
| Feb 16 | Take practice test #1 (now available) |
| Feb 17 – early March | Study weak areas from practice #1 |
| ~Mid-March | Take practice test #2 (readiness check) |
| Late March / early April | **Schedule and sit the real exam** |
| **~June 15** | Your 120-day deadline (the latest you could sit it) |

So yes — once you activate, it's roughly "activation date + 120 days" as your final deadline. If you activate Feb 15, your deadline is around **June 15**, not May 20.

## One thing to double-check in your portal

Some newer GOAA bundles may auto-activate the exam attempt when the course starts, which would change this. When your employer completes the purchase:

1. Log into your SANS account.
2. Find the link to your **GIAC portal** (separate login).
3. Look at your "Certification Attempts" page.
4. See whether GOAA shows as **"Activate" (button to click)** or **"Active – Deadline: [date]"**.

If it's already active when you log in, the clock is already running and you should adjust your study timeline accordingly. If there's an "Activate" button, you control when the clock starts.

## TL;DR

- ✅ Your study sequence (training → index → practice → real exam) is correct.
- ❌ But you can't take practice test #1 *before* activating the attempt.
- ✅ Once activated, you have 120 days for everything: both practice tests + real exam.
- 💡 Activate when you're roughly **2–3 weeks away from wanting to take practice #1**, not earlier.
